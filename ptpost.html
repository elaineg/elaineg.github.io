A Modern Newsmagazine


The Pinetree Post is a student-run publication that releases a new 20-30 page issue every month. It features articles and editorials about upcoming and past school events, as well as community and/or global issues. A fan favourite was an editorial advice column called "Ask Felix", which was written by everybody on staff except for our president, Felix. For my extensive knowledge in print publication design, I was recruited as the layout designer and put in charge of creating each issue with the given articles. On average, each issue took around 20 hours to create. Click layout below for sample issue.

Collaboration, Continuity, Creation


Newspaper layouts are designed to be unobtrusive and completely content focused, so continuity and simplicity of all elements is essential. Teamwork was central to this project: I discussed the appropriate photos and article lengths with each writer, and acted as a liaison between regular journalists and the editor-in-chief. I collaborated with the graphics designer to come up with the defining characteristics of the magazine's look: angular sans-serifs, and straight lines. The lack of added decorations makes for a layout that is clean and easy to read.
