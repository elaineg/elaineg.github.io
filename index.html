Yearbook 2016.

I was given complete creative freedom as the editor-in-chief of my high school yearbook. I began by brainstorming a theme that would reflect some standout events from the school year, such as the retirement of a veteran principal. The result was "Era", a beautiful, 256-page behemoth worthy of coffee table tome status.
Hover over images for more.

Bright Colours, Bold Lines.

The most prominent feature of "Era" was the thick, bold line that ran horizontally across each page - the timeline. Naturally, we displayed the year's events on the timeline in chronological order to simulate an era of time. In addition, each month was themed with a different colour. The obvious divide between sections and the eyeline across every page made for organized, easy-to-read content.

Dynamic Photos.

First and foremost, the quality of a yearbook is determined by its photos. We put a special emphasis on curating photos that displayed either motion or emotion. To capture these challenging spur-of-the-moment shots, we utilized advanced camera techniques such as panning and bokeh. The resulting photos are so crisp and candid, they come to life on the pages.

The End of an Era.

I wanted the cover to reflect both the school's student body and to introduce the theme. Taking a minimalistic approach, I left the cover entirely blank safe for "Era" on the front and "The End" on the back. I joined the two phrases with a subtle timeline to form the book's tagline, "The end of an era". Around these words I simply pasted greetings in different languages to reflect the ethnic diversity of our school's students.
