I'm going to make personal finance accessible for the masses

Why I care

I forgot to file for tax returns last year. I have no idea how to pay for my student loans. I'm probably a little dependent on my credit card. Having completed two years of a finance-heavy business degree, I figured I should be in a good position to manage these basic personal finance areas. But I'm not.

Given my lack of knowledge, I felt there was a good chance that my peers felt equally as unprepared to handle their presonal finances following 13 years of mandatory education. So I did some research.

During my internship at Finn AI, I surveyed students to gauge financial preparedness within my peers. I found that students from wealthier backgrounds were more informed about personal finance management than those from lower socioeconomic backgrounds. This creates a cycle where wealthier students stayed wealthy while poorer students struggle to reach financial stability.

What I'm doing

During my first year on QTMA as a product analyst, I was tasked to select an industry and argue why it would be first to be disrupted by technology. I chose to present a case for the financial services industry, and was validated when a panel of industry experts agreed it was most ripe for disruption. I am convinced that technology will democratize finance and alleviate financial inequality, so I plan to build my career in fintechs that share my vision.

At Finn AI, I created accessible content to educate Gen Z about basic financial topics. Eventually, Finn AI hopes to incorporate financial literacy content into their core product. I researched attitudes toward financial topics and I am more informed about when and why people seek to supplement their financial education. These learnings will inform how I develop financial literacy solutions in the future.
